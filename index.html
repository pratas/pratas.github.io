Diogo Pratas is an Assistant Researcher at IEETA/DETI at the University of Aveiro. His research interests include computational biology, data compression, and information theory. He earned his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Aveiro and holds a B.S. degree in Information and Communications Technologies also from the University of Aveiro, with a segment carried at the Pontifical University of Salamanca. He joined IEETA/DETI in 2009, after being working some years in the private sector. Diogo actively collaborates with the Department of Virology at the University of Helsinki, namely in the identification and reconstruction of viral genomes, and the respective human-hosts, at multi-organ level. He is a member of the Super Dimension Fortress, the International Society for Computational Biology, and the Portuguese Association for Pattern Recognition.

Tools

  • TRACESPipe: a pipeline for reconstruction of viral and human-host genomes at multi-organ level.
  • FALCON-meta: a sensitive and ultra-fast tool to infer metagenomic sample composition.
  • Cryfa: a secure encryption tool for genomic data.
  • Smash: a tool to find and visualize rearrangements in DNA sequences.
  • Chester: a memory efficient tool to map and visualize evolutionary regions.
  • GTO: a multi-purpose toolkit for genomics.
  • GeCo: an efficient compressor for genomic sequences (reference and reference-free).
  • AC: a compressor for Amino Acid sequences (reference and reference-free).
  • MFCompress: an efficient compressor for FASTA sequences.
  • GReEn: a reference-based compressor for resequencing data.
  • MAGNET: an alignment-free tool to separate targeted data of FASTQ reads.
  • EAGLE: an alignment-free tool to compute (minimal) Relative Absent Words.
